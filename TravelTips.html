Get prepared with these essential travel tips before your trip!

1. Plan for the Weather

The best time to visit is December to April for the west and south coasts, and May to September for the east coast.

2. Embrace the Culture

When visiting sacred sites in Kandy, Adam's Peak wear modest clothing, remove shoes, and avoid loud conversations.

3. Respect Wildlife & Nature

In places like Yala National Park, keep a safe distance from animals and avoid loud noises.

4. Enjoy the Coastlines

From the pristine Marble Beach to the golden shores of Mirissa and Unawatuna, Sri Lanka offers incredible beach escapes.

Place Why Visit?
Kandy Kandy, blends spirituality, nature and culture with the Temple of the Tooth Relic, serene Kandy Lake, vibrant dances and lush botnical gardens.
Galle Galle captivates with its UNESCO-listed Dutch Fort, stunning ocean views, rich history and charming cafes.
Sigiriya Sigiriya, a majestic UNESCO-listes rock fortress, dazzles with panoramic views, ancient frescoes and the iconic Lion's Paw entrance.
Ella Ella, a serene mountain escape, enchants with lush landscapes, waterfalls, tea plantation, the iconic Nine Arches Bridge and scenic hikes.
Yala National Park Yala National Park, Sri Lanka's top wildlife haven, offers thrilling jeep safaris to spot leopards, elephants, crocodiles and diverse birdlife.
Adam's Peak Adam's Peak, a sacred pilgrimage site, offers a spiritually rewarding hike with breaktaking sunrise views.
Marble Beach Marble Beach, A pristine paradise in Sri Lanka, where calm waters invite serene swims and vibrant snorkeling. Just a splash away from Pigeon Island’s diving wonders.


Place Important Instructions
Kandy Dress modestly at temples, expect vibrant crowds during Esala Perahera.
